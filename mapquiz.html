Map Quiz

Countries of the world quiz

My Role

I created this app as part of my Udacity iOS Nanodegree. I was the sole designer and developer.

Product Goals

Create a better country quiz app to use on commute/spare 5 mins to get better at countries of the world

The idea

I spend a reasonable amount of time on Sporkle with friends. It is a desktop quiz app. It has all sorts of categories but the section I use is the Geography section, more specifically learning countries, cities and states of thw world and where they are. Looking on the app store for a mobile version that I could use on my commute, or in a quiet 5 mins waiting for something, I was not impressed with the apps that existed, so I set out to make my own. Sporkle also now has its own app, but for counties of the world it does not show the user a map of the countries, rather it just asks you to type in all the countries in the world/by continent!

User Validation

To validate that what I thought of as bad apps other people also thought they were bad apps, I collected three other apps that did the same thing and asked users to tell me what they liked and didn't like about them. As well as showing them the Sporkle desktop game.

The apps I got them to compare were:

  • Sporkle App for countries
  • Maps of Our World
  • World Map Challenge
  • Maps of All Countries

Analysis

What users liked:

  • Able to tap countries to select
  • Being asked all of a continent to add context
  • Seeing a map of the continent
  • Being asked all of a continent to add context
  • Practice and test mode
  • Cities, capitals and states too

What users didn't like:

  • Slow animations preventing them from moving forward
  • Not having country marked as guessed once correctly guessed—what to see progress
  • Do not like multiple makes it harder to learn

The app that won in usability was Maps of Our World and the least liked by quite some margin was the Sporkle app. Users felt there was no point in Sporkle app—it just asked them to re-gurgitate a list country names—there was no way to practice the countries.

Taking the likes we decided to run with them to create an app to deal with all the features users liked
